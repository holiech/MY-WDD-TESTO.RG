The Divine Principle of Charity: The Pure Love of Christ

Dear friends,

Today, we delve into the profound concept of charity, also known as the pure love of Christ. Charity is more than just an act of kindness or a feeling of benevolence. It embodies the very essence of Christ's teachings and His divine nature. The Savior Himself declared that charity is the greatest of all virtues:

"And charity suffereth long, and is kind, and envieth not, and is not puffed up, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil, and rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth, beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things. Wherefore, my beloved brethren, if ye have not charity, ye are nothing, for charity never faileth. Wherefore, cleave unto charity, which is the greatest of all, for all things must fail—but charity is the pure love of Christ, and it endureth forever; and whoso is found possessed of it at the last day, it shall be well with him."
Moroni 7:45-47

This scripture from the Book of Mormon beautifully encapsulates the attributes of charity. It goes beyond surface-level kindness and encompasses patience, humility, selflessness, and a genuine concern for the welfare of others. Charity, as defined by Christ, transforms our actions and motivations. It is the driving force behind true discipleship.

Christ's life exemplified charity in every sense. His selfless sacrifice on the cross was the ultimate act of love. He healed the sick, comforted the broken-hearted, and ministered to the marginalized. His interactions were marked by compassion and empathy, setting a perfect example for us to follow.

As we strive to develop charity, we align ourselves with the Savior's teachings. We learn to forgive, to serve, and to see the divine potential in every individual. Our hearts soften, and our perspectives expand. We seek to lift others' burdens, to mourn with those who mourn, and to share in their joys. In essence, charity empowers us to become instruments of God's love.

Moreover, the connection between charity and faith is profound. The Apostle Paul taught that charity "worketh by love" and that without charity, even faith and knowledge are incomplete (see Galatians 5:6 and 1 Corinthians 13:2). Charity is the catalyst that breathes life into our spiritual endeavors.

In conclusion, charity—Christ's pure love—is a divine attribute that transcends cultural boundaries, personal differences, and challenges. It is an invitation to see others through the Savior's eyes and to serve as He served. Let us strive to cultivate charity in our hearts, allowing it to guide our actions and shape our character. By so doing, we draw closer to the heart of the Savior and fulfill His commandment to "love one another; as I have loved you" (John 13:34).

Thank you for joining me in this exploration of the principle of charity. May our lives be infused with the pure love of Christ, and may we continue to extend that love to all those around us.

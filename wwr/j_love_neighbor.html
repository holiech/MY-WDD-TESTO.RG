A Love for Our Neighbor

My dear brothers and sisters, as we gather today, I am humbled by the opportunity to reflect on a principle that resonates deeply within our souls—the principle of love for our neighbor. In a world often marked by division, confusion, and turmoil, the call to love and uplift one another stands as a beacon of hope and a testament to the teachings of our Savior.

1. The Greatest Commandment: In the sacred words of Matthew 22:37-39, we are reminded of the paramount commandment given by our Lord: "'Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind.' This is the first and great commandment. And the second is like unto it, 'Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.'" The echo of these words reminds us that our faith is rooted in love—in love for God and love for each other.

2. The Good Samaritan: The poignant tale of the Good Samaritan, found in Luke 10:25-37, captures the essence of a heartfelt love for our neighbor. It speaks to the call within us to be more than bystanders—to be compassionate responders to the needs of those around us. The Samaritan's selfless actions resonate across time, showing us that love is an action that transcends social boundaries and compels us to step out of our comfort zones.

3. Service as an Expression of Love: The Apostle John tenderly reminds us in 1 John 3:18, "My little children, let us not love in word, neither in tongue; but in deed and in truth." These words touch our hearts, imploring us to turn our love into tangible acts of kindness and service. Love, at its core, is about action—about reaching out to those in need, about standing by someone's side, about making a difference in the lives of others.

4. Recognizing Christ in Others: How profoundly beautiful it is to consider that, through our love for our neighbors, we are, in essence, loving our Savior. In Matthew 25:40, Jesus assures us, "Verily I say unto you, inasmuch as ye have done it unto one of the least of these my brethren, ye have done it unto me." When we uplift and care for others, we are extending our love to Christ Himself.

5. Overcoming Divisions: In a world that often emphasizes our differences, the clarion call to love our neighbor can mend the fractures that divide us. The Apostle Paul, in Galatians 5:14, encapsulates this truth: "For all the law is fulfilled in one word, even in this; Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself." Our heartfelt love can dissolve barriers and bring us together as one human family.

6. A Book of Mormon Invitation: The Book of Mormon, a companion to the Bible, echoes the call to love and serve. In Mosiah 2:17, King Benjamin teaches, "And behold, I tell you these things that ye may learn wisdom; that ye may learn that when ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God." The Book of Mormon testifies that our love for others is a sacred expression of our devotion to God.

7. Love as a Transformative Force: In the journey of discipleship, love becomes a transformative force. It shapes our hearts, softens our attitudes, and breaks down the barriers of prejudice. The Savior's example beckons us to rise above the trivialities that divide us and instead unite in the common bond of love.

My dear friends, let us not underestimate the power of a heartfelt love for our neighbor. It is not a fleeting emotion; it is a transformative force that, when embraced, can change lives and shape communities. The call to love our neighbor is a call to a higher plane of existence—one that resonates with the very nature of our Savior, who is the embodiment of pure love.

As we live out this love each day, let it be a testament to our commitment to Christ's teachings. In our acts of kindness, in our moments of service, in our words of encouragement, let us find the divine purpose of our existence—to truly love and uplift one another.

May our hearts be touched and transformed by this heartfelt love for our neighbor. In the name of Jesus Christ, amen.
